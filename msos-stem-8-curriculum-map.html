Curriculum Map by Unit

Each unit lists its standards, learning objectives, essential content, pacing, assessments, and resources.

Unit Standards Standards Reference Learning Objectives Essential Content & Skills Sequencing & Pacing Assessments Resources Used
Unit 1: 🔬 I am a Scientist and an Engineer MS-ETS + MS-PS + MYP Sci MS-ETS1-1 (define problems); MS-ETS1-2 (evaluate solutions); MS-PS1-4 (states of matter); MYP Crit. A/B/C 1. Apply the Scientific Method and Engineering Design Process to physical science; 2. Master lab safety and probe-ware; 3. Investigate mass, volume, density, and heat energy. Lab safety; scientific method; engineering design process; variables and hypotheses; properties of matter (mass, volume, density); heat energy; introduction to probe-ware; lab report format. Q1: 4 weeks
(8/3 – 8/28)		 Properties of Matter Lab (Crit. B/C); lab safety quiz. Innovations Lab probe-ware (temperature, motion); digital balances; graduated cylinders; PhET States of Matter.
Unit 2: ⚗️ A Period Matters! MS-PS1 + MYP Sci MS-PS1-1 (atomic composition); MS-PS1-2 (chemical reactions); MS-PS1-3 (synthetic materials); MS-PS1-4 (kinetic energy); MS-PS1-5 (conservation of mass); MS-PS1-6 (thermal energy in reactions); MYP Crit. A/B/C/D 1. Read the periodic table; 2. Model atomic structure; 3. Balance chemical equations; 4. Investigate acids, bases, and polymers; 5. Apply stoichiometry. Periodic table structure; atomic models (Bohr, electron cloud); types of chemical reactions; conservation of matter; acids and bases; stoichiometry basics; polymers; field study methods. Q1-Q2: 14 weeks
(8/31 – 12/18)		 Element Babybook (Crit. A/D); Berth Project (Crit. A/D); Stain Lab (Crit. B/C). PhET Build an Atom; PhET Balancing Chemical Equations; classroom chemistry kits; pH strips; molecular model kits.
Unit 3: 🚀 Forces and Motion MS-PS2 + MS-PS3 + MYP Sci MS-PS2-1 (Newton's 3rd law); MS-PS2-2 (Newton's 2nd law); MS-PS2-3 (magnetic forces); MS-PS2-4 (gravitational force); MS-PS2-5 (fields at a distance); MYP Crit. A/B/C/D 1. State and apply Newton's Laws of Motion; 2. Compute velocity and acceleration; 3. Investigate projectile motion; 4. Use simple machines. Newton's Three Laws; balanced vs. unbalanced forces; velocity and acceleration; Newton's Universal Law of Gravity; simple machines; projectile motion; conservation of momentum. Q3: 10 weeks
(1/7 – 3/12)		 The Apprentices Project (Crit. A/B/C/D); Velocity Lab, Acceleration Lab, Projectile Lab. PhET Forces and Motion Basics; PhET Projectile Motion; Innovations Lab simple machines kits; motion sensors and dynamics carts.
Unit 4: ⚡ Faraday's Fundamental Forces MS-PS2 + MS-PS3 + MYP Sci MS-PS3-1 (kinetic energy); MS-PS3-2 (potential energy); MS-PS3-3 (thermal energy transfer); MS-PS3-4 (temperature and particle motion); MS-PS3-5 (energy transfer); MS-PS2-3 (magnetic and electric forces); MYP Crit. A/B/C/D 1. Explore the electricity-magnetism relationship; 2. Apply Ohm's Law; 3. Analyze series and parallel circuits; 4. Debate AI and robotics in modern society. Electric current; electric circuits; Ohm's Law (V = I × R); series and parallel circuits; magnetism; electromagnetic induction; electric power generation; fundamentals of AI and robotics. Q4: 12 weeks
(3/29 – 6/3)		 Critical Lab Skills Assessment (Crit. B/C); Faraday Final Exam (Crit. A/D). PhET Circuit Construction Kit; PhET Faraday's Electromagnetic Lab; Innovations Lab circuit kits; multimeters; robotics kits.
