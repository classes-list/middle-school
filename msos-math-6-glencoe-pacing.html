Mary, Star of the Sea 6th-grade math is accelerated — students use a textbook normally taught a year later. We resequence the Glencoe chapters so that math is ready exactly when STEM (science) class needs it. Below is the actual quarter-by-quarter map of our syllabus, with the Glencoe chapter that each unit draws from, the custom Hawaii- and lab-themed lessons we add, and the STEM work the math directly supports.

Why we resequence The Glencoe table of contents goes ratios → percents → integers → equations → geometry. Our STEM calendar needs density in September, universe-scale numbers in November, Earth-time and rates in February, and climate statistics in April. So we land Glencoe Ch 4 (decimals) in Q1 — paired with our custom Density and Percent Error lessons — and reorder the rest to match. Glencoe chapter tests are still administered in their original form.
