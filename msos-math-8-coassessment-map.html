Math 8 · Co-Assessment Map

Joint capstone projects with STEM · Glencoe Algebra 1 · SY 2026–2027

← Back to syllabus
Interdisciplinary Co-Assessment. Four times a year, one project is graded by both teachers using their own subject's rubric. Students submit one deliverable; it earns one grade in math and one in STEM. The math rubric measures mathematical correctness, problem-solving process, and quantitative reasoning. The STEM rubric measures scientific understanding, inquiry & design, processing, and reflection on impacts. One project, two grades, two perspectives.
📦 One shared artifact 🧮 Math rubric (navy) 🔬 STEM rubric (gold) 🤝 Teacher coordination
Q1

Lab Math & Number Foundation

Late September submission
JOINT CAPSTONE — ONE PROJECT, TWO GRADES
Properties of Matter Forensics Lab

📦 Shared Deliverable

A 5-page forensic-style lab report identifying 5 unknown materials by density, with significant figures, percent error, and a reference-table cross-check.

🧮 Math Rubric

A — Knowing & UnderstandingCorrectly applies D = m/V with significant figures; uses scientific notation.
B — Inquiring & DesigningMeasurement protocol matches sample type; uncertainty considered.
C — Processing & EvaluatingPercent error computed correctly; sig figs respected throughout.
D — CommunicatingLab report is precise; arithmetic shown.

🔬 STEM Rubric

A — Knowing & UnderstandingExplains density as an intensive material property; distinguishes materials by additional tests.
B — Inquiring & DesigningForensic identification logic is sound; ambiguous cases handled with secondary tests.
C — Processing & EvaluatingData interpretation is rigorous; error sources catalogued.
D — Reflecting on ImpactsConsiders real-world forensic, manufacturing, or environmental applications.

🤝 Teacher Coordination

Math teaches sig figs, percent error, and scientific notation in August. STEM provides the unknown samples and supervises the lab in September.

PURE-MATH COMPANION (math grade only): Tsunami Travel-Time Calculator
Q2

Lines, Slopes & Stoichiometry

Mid-December submission
JOINT CAPSTONE — ONE PROJECT, TWO GRADES
Element Babybook

📦 Shared Deliverable

A single illustrated "babybook" for one element, integrating weighted-average atomic mass, isotope-abundance percents, a linear-equation property model, periodic-table coordinates, and the chemistry/biology of the element.

🧮 Math Rubric

A — Knowing & UnderstandingWeighted-average atomic mass computed correctly; linear equation for property vs. atomic number is accurate.
B — Inquiring & DesigningIsotope data sourced and presented accurately; periodic-table coordinate map is correct.
C — Processing & EvaluatingPredictions from linear-regression equation are tested or sanity-checked.
D — CommunicatingQuantitative claims supported by computation; units consistent.

🔬 STEM Rubric

A — Knowing & UnderstandingExplains atomic structure, isotopes, and the element's chemistry.
B — Inquiring & DesigningElement "life story" weaves quantitative and qualitative information.
C — Processing & EvaluatingProperty data interpreted in chemical context.
D — Reflecting on ImpactsDiscusses the element's role in biology, industry, or Hawai‘i (e.g., titanium in lava).

🤝 Teacher Coordination

Single jointly designed project. Math teaches weighted averages and linear regression in November. STEM provides element-research scaffolding. ONE final book — graded twice.

PURE-MATH COMPANION (math grade only): Catamaran Load Curve Model
Q3

Math for Motion (Front-Loaded)

Mid-February submission
JOINT CAPSTONE — ONE PROJECT, TWO GRADES
The Apprentices Project — Marble Run Engineering

📦 Shared Deliverable

A physical working marble run with a defined landing target + engineering log documenting velocity, slope, projectile range, and Pythagorean diagonal calculations + a 10-trial test report.

🧮 Math Rubric

A — Knowing & UnderstandingCorrectly applies v = d/t; projectile range R = v²sin(2θ)/g; Pythagorean theorem.
B — Inquiring & DesigningRun design choices justified mathematically.
C — Processing & EvaluatingTrial data analyzed correctly; iterations measured.
D — CommunicatingEngineering log is precise; calculations clearly shown.

🔬 STEM Rubric

A — Knowing & UnderstandingExplains kinematics (velocity, acceleration, projectile motion).
B — Inquiring & DesigningEngineering design process followed; iterations documented.
C — Processing & EvaluatingEnergy conversion across the run analyzed; friction considered.
D — Reflecting on ImpactsConsiders real engineering applications: launches, trajectories, safety.

🤝 Teacher Coordination

Math teaches motion equations in early January (front-loaded). STEM facilitates the build and 10-trial test in late January/early February.

PURE-MATH COMPANION (math grade only): Surf Wave Geometry Photo Analysis
Q4

Electricity, Waves & Cosmos

Mid-May submission
JOINT CAPSTONE — ONE PROJECT, TWO GRADES
Faraday Project — Hawai‘i Charging Station

📦 Shared Deliverable

A design portfolio (or working prototype) for a solar/wind phone charging station, with circuit schematic, Ohm's-Law calculations, inverter wavelength using inverse variation, watt-hour budget, and a 10-year cost analysis. The portfolio is used as the integrated practical assessment for STEM's Faraday Final.

🧮 Math Rubric

A — Knowing & UnderstandingCorrectly applies V = IR; linear-function modeling; inverse variation f × λ = c.
B — Inquiring & DesigningCircuit design choices justified; sun-hours data sourced.
C — Processing & EvaluatingWatt-hour calculations correct; payback period accurate.
D — CommunicatingSchematic is readable; cost analysis is clear.

🔬 STEM Rubric

A — Knowing & UnderstandingExplains electricity, magnetism, and energy conversion.
B — Inquiring & DesigningRenewable-energy design respects safety and feasibility.
C — Processing & EvaluatingCost-benefit analysis is rigorous.
D — Reflecting on ImpactsConsiders Hawai‘i renewable-energy mandate, energy equity, and sustainability.

🤝 Teacher Coordination

Math teaches Ohm's Law and inverse variation in April. STEM uses the portfolio as the practical component of the Faraday Final. ONE artifact, dual graded.

PURE-MATH COMPANION (math grade only): Orbital Sundial or Pinhole Telescope Build
