🎯 7th Grade Goal — Expanding Perspectives

By the end of 7th grade, students will:

  • Analyze major American civilizations and cultures across different regions, identifying political, social, and economic systems.
  • Evaluate the causes and effects of key historical events using evidence from primary and secondary sources.
  • Compare different perspectives on historical issues and explain how they influence interpretation.
  • Strengthen research and presentation skills by using evidence to support historical claims.

📚 Areas of Concentration — Grade 7

Ancient American cultures to colonization of America to the American Civil War.

  • Unit 1 — Ancient American Cultures (Maya, Aztec, Inca, North American Indigenous)
  • Unit 2 — Age of Exploration & Early Colonization
  • Unit 3 — The 13 Colonies & Colonial Life
  • Unit 4 — The American Revolution & New Nation
  • Unit 5 — Westward Expansion & the Civil War

📰 AOW — Article of the Week (15%)

5 points each. A weekly writing response students complete on their Google Drive account.

A poem, story, or article will be given on Mondays. Your response is due on Fridays.

Your response should include:

  1. Any part of the article you liked, disliked, or were confused about.
  2. WHY did you like, dislike, or were confused about this part.
  3. Length is not as important as the amount of details and explanations you write about.

📝 Classwork — Summaries & Notes (30%)

4 points each. To show me that you understand the information you received in class, you may be asked to write a summary about the material.

Sometimes, the summaries will be counted as a quiz.

✍️ Essays

You will be given a grading rubric before the essays are given. When we begin a unit, you will be given an essential question for the essay.

We will be working on our answers to the essential questions together throughout the unit.

🎨 Projects (30%)

25 points each. Projects start during the 2nd quarter.

Projects follow the same guidelines as the essays — rubric provided ahead of time, anchored to a unit’s essential question.

📝 Quizzes (15%)

5–10 points each. Used to check understanding throughout each unit.

🤝 Active Learning (10%)

2 points each class day. Participation is based on coming to class prepared:

  • Macbook charged
  • Learning materials prepared
  • Assignments completed
  • Ready for class discussions
  • Being an attentive listener
  • Actively engaged in class

📅 What happens if I turn in an assignment late?

Work not completed and turned in by the assigned date is considered late.

If a student is absent on the date work is due, the assignment is due within a week from absence.

There is no penalty in your homework grade for an assignment turned in late, as long as it is turned in. However, there will be a deduction in your Active Learning grade.

If you do not turn in an assignment, your score will not be added into your grade.

A late assignment is better than a missing assignment.
