📋 Course Description

This course is based on the ministry of catechesis for religious education and faith formation: believe, worship, live, and pray. Students learn the doctrine of the Catholic Church and establish a common language of faith, engage in understanding the sacramental life of the Catholic Church, focus attention on the moral teachings of the Church, and experience a wide variety of prayers. Students will develop a deeper understanding of faith and greater love for God.

Schoolwide Learning Expectation (SLE): MSOS students will become active Christians who respect others, make good choices, value prayer, and practice the teachings of the Catholic Church.

🙏 How We Nurture Our Faith in Religion Class

  • Praying and singing hymns
  • Reading Scripture and memorizing Bible verses
  • Praying from the Book of Psalms
  • Writing “Dear God” journal entries
  • Reflecting on the Readings and Gospel of the Day
  • Discussing activities from the Blest Are We textbook
  • Role-playing Bible stories (e.g., the Christmas story)
  • Learning about the Lives of Saints as models of faith
  • Praying the Rosary, Stations of the Cross, and other devotions
  • Integrating Religion and Art by illustrating Bible verses
  • Participating in the Sacraments and Liturgical Year as church ministers (lectors, choir, altar servers)
