Math 7 · Co-Assessment Map

Joint capstone projects with STEM · Glencoe Math Accelerated (Pre-Algebra) · SY 2026–2027

← Back to syllabus
Interdisciplinary Co-Assessment. Four times a year, one project is graded by both teachers using their own subject's rubric. Students submit one deliverable; it earns one grade in math and one in STEM. The math rubric measures mathematical correctness, problem-solving process, and quantitative reasoning. The STEM rubric measures scientific understanding, inquiry & design, processing, and reflection on impacts. Same project, two grades, two perspectives.

Q1

Lab Math Refresher & Foundations

Mid-September submission
JOINT CAPSTONE
📐 Hawaiian Watershed Water Budget ↔ 🔬 Hawaiian Water Cycle Poster

📦 Shared Deliverable

A hydrology report with watershed map, annual water-budget table, unit-converted flow rates, percent error vs. USGS published values, and a visual poster of the water cycle for the chosen watershed.

🧮 Math Rubric

A — Knowing & UnderstandingCorrectly applies proportional reasoning; converts units across cfs, L/min, m³/s.
B — Inquiring & DesigningData sourcing is appropriate; chosen watershed is justified.
C — Processing & EvaluatingPercent calculations and percent error are computed accurately; water budget balances.
D — CommunicatingHydrology report is well-organized; units used consistently.

🔬 STEM Rubric

A — Knowing & UnderstandingExplains the water cycle phases and watershed-aquifer relationship.
B — Inquiring & DesigningWatershed map labels rainfall paths, runoff, recharge accurately.
C — Processing & EvaluatingInterprets quantitative data in ecological context.
D — Reflecting on ImpactsConsiders human impact (urbanization, agriculture) on the watershed.

🤝 Teacher Coordination

Math hosts the unit-conversion and percent-error refresher in early September. STEM facilitates poster design and watershed selection. Both teachers grade the combined deliverable.

PURE-MATH COMPANION (math grade only): Microscopic World Scale Trading Cards
Q2

Brain Data + Tools for Cells

Mid-December submission (early January for STEM cell unit)
JOINT CAPSTONE
📐 Build-a-Cell with SA:V Calculations ↔ 🔬 Cell Analogy Poster

📦 Shared Deliverable

A 3D physical cell model with measurements; a technical report with SA, V, and SA:V calculations for the whole cell and three organelles; an analogy poster explaining each cell part by comparison to a familiar system.

🧮 Math Rubric

A — Knowing & UnderstandingCorrectly applies SA = 4πr² and V = (4/3)πr³; computes SA:V ratios.
B — Inquiring & DesigningCell model dimensions are reasonable; organelles scaled appropriately.
C — Processing & EvaluatingCalculations are correct; rearranged formulas applied to diffusion prediction.
D — CommunicatingTechnical report shows clear work; ratios are interpreted in context.

🔬 STEM Rubric

A — Knowing & UnderstandingExplains each organelle's function correctly.
B — Inquiring & DesigningAnalogy is structurally accurate and creatively chosen.
C — Processing & EvaluatingLinks SA:V quantitative finding to the biological "why cells stay small" concept.
D — Reflecting on ImpactsConsiders what changes when cells grow too large or differentiate (cancer, multicellularity).

🤝 Teacher Coordination

Math teaches SA:V ratio in late November. STEM provides the analogy-poster framework in December. Joint submission before winter break.

PURE-MATH COMPANION (math grade only): Screen Time vs. Sleep Class Study
Q3

Cells, Geometry & Inequalities

Mid-February submission
JOINT CAPSTONE
📐 Outbreak Spread Simulation ↔ 🔬 Virus Debate

📦 Shared Deliverable

A spreadsheet exponential-growth model with intervention scenarios + a 2-page policy memo + debate preparation (math team provides quantitative evidence to both sides).

🧮 Math Rubric

A — Knowing & UnderstandingCorrectly applies exponential growth (cases = N₀ × R^t); probability of transmission per contact.
B — Inquiring & DesigningModel parameters justified; intervention scenarios plausible.
C — Processing & EvaluatingHospital-capacity inequality solved correctly; graphs accurately labeled.
D — CommunicatingPolicy memo translates math correctly into actionable recommendations.

🔬 STEM Rubric

A — Knowing & UnderstandingUnderstands viral structure, replication, and transmission.
B — Inquiring & DesigningDebate position is supported with both quantitative and biological evidence.
C — Processing & EvaluatingCounter-arguments addressed; evidence is sourced.
D — Reflecting on ImpactsConsiders public-health ethics: individual liberty vs. collective safety.

🤝 Teacher Coordination

Math builds the simulation model in late January. STEM facilitates the debate in mid-February. The simulation model is shared evidence for both sides.

PURE-MATH COMPANION (math grade only): Marine Protected Area Geometry Design
Q4

Probability for Genetics

Mid-May submission
JOINT CAPSTONE
📐 Mock Pedigree Probability Project ↔ 🔬 Sesame Street Genetics

📦 Shared Deliverable

A multi-generation fictional-family pedigree chart with Punnett squares for 3 traits and compound probabilities, paired with a kid-friendly explainer (poster or short video) that teaches heredity using Sesame-Street characters.

🧮 Math Rubric

A — Knowing & UnderstandingPunnett squares for autosomal-dominant, X-linked, and co-dominant traits constructed correctly.
B — Inquiring & DesigningPedigree chart is internally consistent across generations.
C — Processing & EvaluatingCompound-probability calculations correct (independent events multiplied).
D — CommunicatingProbabilities reported with appropriate notation; uncertainty acknowledged.

🔬 STEM Rubric

A — Knowing & UnderstandingExplains dominant/recessive, X-linked, and co-dominant inheritance correctly.
B — Inquiring & DesigningSesame-Street explainer is age-appropriate and accurate.
C — Processing & EvaluatingTrait inheritance through generations interpreted correctly.
D — Reflecting on ImpactsConsiders ethics of genetic counseling and family planning.

🤝 Teacher Coordination

Math owns the probability + Punnett-square instruction in April. STEM facilitates the Sesame-Street character casting and explainer production in early May.

PURE-MATH COMPANION (math grade only): Hawaiian Monk Seal Population Model
