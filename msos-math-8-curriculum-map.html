Beginning of School - Lab Math & Number Foundations CCSS 8.NS.A.1 (Rational vs. irrational); 8.NS.A.2 (Approximate irrationals); 8.EE.A.1 (Integer exponent rules); 8.EE.A.2 (Square root & cube root symbols); 8.EE.A.3 (Scientific notation for large/small); 8.EE.A.4 (Operations with scientific notation) 1. Use sig figs and percent error in lab measurements; 2. Convert metric units with multi-step rate conversions; 3. Multiply and divide numbers in scientific notation; 4. Classify rationals vs. irrationals; 5. Apply integer-exponent rules. Significant Figures, Percent Error, Unit Conversions (KHDUdcm staircase), Scientific Notation (operations), Rational vs. Irrational Numbers, Square Roots & Cube Roots, Exponent Laws (product, quotient, power, negative, zero). Quarter 1: 5 Weeks (8/3-9/4) 1. Sig figs & percent error quiz; 2. Scientific notation operations quiz; 3. Real number system quiz; 4. Exponent laws quiz; 5. Lab Math Bootcamp report. PhET: Estimation, Build an Atom. MSOS Math 8 site: Lab Math & Number Foundations units. Hands-on: digital scales, calculators.
Lines & Slopes CCSS 8.EE.B.5 (Graph proportional relationships, interpret slope); 8.EE.B.6 (Slope using similar triangles; y=mx and y=mx+b); 8.EE.C.7 (Solve linear equations); 8.EE.C.7b (Variables on both sides) 1. Compute slope from graphs, tables, equations, and two points; 2. Write linear equations in slope-intercept form; 3. Solve multi-step linear equations (variables both sides, special solutions); 4. Rearrange formulas to solve for a given variable. Slope (rise/run), Slope from Two Points, Slope-Intercept Form (y = mx + b), Standard Form, Point-Slope Form, Multi-Step Linear Equations, Special Solution Cases (no solution, infinitely many), Rearranging Formulas (Density, Speed, Ohm's Law). Quarter 1: 4 Weeks (9/8-10/2) 1. Slope quiz; 2. Slope-intercept form quiz; 3. Multi-step equations quiz; 4. Rearranging formulas quiz; 5. Quarter 1 test. PhET: Graphing Lines, Graphing Slope-Intercept, Equation Grapher. MSOS Math 8 site: Lines & Slopes unit. Function Joystick interactive.
Systems of Equations & Stoichiometry CCSS 8.EE.C.8 (Pairs of simultaneous linear equations); 8.EE.C.8a (Solutions correspond to intersection); 8.EE.C.8b (Solve by inspection, graphing, algebra); 8.EE.C.8c (Real-world problems); 8.NS.A.1 (Weighted averages with rationals) 1. Solve systems of equations by graphing, substitution, elimination; 2. Identify systems with 0, 1, or infinite solutions; 3. Apply systems to chemistry stoichiometry; 4. Compute weighted averages for isotope abundance. Systems of Equations (graphing, substitution, elimination), Number of Solutions, Real-World Systems Problems, Weighted Averages (Atomic Mass), Percent & Weighted Means, Chemistry Stoichiometry connection. Project: Element Babybook. Quarter 2: 9 Weeks (10/19-12/18) 1. Systems by graphing quiz; 2. Substitution quiz; 3. Elimination quiz; 4. Weighted averages quiz; 5. Element Babybook project; 6. Quarter 2 test. PhET: Graphing Lines, Reactants Products and Leftovers, Balancing Chemical Equations. MSOS Math 8 site: Systems & Stoichiometry units. Hands-on: M&M isotope activity.
Functions, Motion & Pythagorean Theorem CCSS 8.F.A.1 (Function as rule); 8.F.A.2 (Compare functions); 8.F.A.3 (y=mx+b is linear); 8.F.B.4 (Model linear relationship); 8.F.B.5 (Describe function behavior); 8.G.A.1-4 (Transformations & similarity); 8.G.A.5 (Angle sum, exterior angles); 8.G.B.6-8 (Pythagorean theorem & distance); 8.EE.C.7 (Linear equations) 1. Define functions and use function notation; 2. Distinguish linear from nonlinear functions; 3. Construct linear functions to model physical motion; 4. Apply the Pythagorean Theorem in 2D and 3D; 5. Perform rigid transformations and dilations on the coordinate plane. Function Definition, Linear vs. Nonlinear, Function Notation, Physics Equations of Motion (v = d/t, d = ½at²), Pythagorean Theorem (a² + b² = c²), Distance Formula, Intro Trigonometry (SOH-CAH-TOA), Transformations (translation, reflection, rotation, dilation), Angle Relationships (parallel lines & transversals, triangle angle sum, exterior angle). Quarter 3: 9 Weeks (1/7-3/12) 1. Functions quiz; 2. Physics Motion Lab; 3. Pythagorean theorem quiz; 4. Trig intro quiz; 5. Transformations quiz; 6. Angle relationships quiz; 7. Quarter 3 test. PhET: Function Builder, Vector Addition, Projectile Motion, Pythagorean Theorem. MSOS Math 8 site: Linear Function unit, Function Joystick. Hands-on: projectile lab setup.
Electricity, Waves & Optics CCSS 8.EE.B.5 (Proportional relationships in physics); 8.EE.A.4 (Scientific notation in physical calculations); 8.G.B.7 (Pythagorean Theorem in 2D/3D applied to Snell's Law geometry) 1. Apply Ohm's Law (V = IR) using linear functions; 2. Analyze series and parallel circuits; 3. Use v = fλ to relate wave speed, frequency, wavelength; 4. Apply Snell's Law to refraction problems. Ohm's Law as Linear Function, Series Circuits, Parallel Circuits, Equivalent Resistance, Wave Speed (v = fλ), Frequency & Wavelength (inverse variation), Snell's Law (n₁sin θ₁ = n₂sin θ₂), Refraction Angle problems. Lab: Bulbs & Batteries; Ripple Tank. Quarter 4: 5 Weeks (3/29-4/30) 1. Ohm's Law quiz; 2. Circuits lab; 3. Wave equation quiz; 4. Snell's Law quiz; 5. Optics project. PhET: Circuit Construction Kit, Waves on a String, Wave Interference, Bending Light. MSOS Math 8 site: Electricity & Optics units.
Cosmos, Geometry & Capstone CCSS 8.EE.A.2 (Square & cube roots applied to Kepler's Laws); 8.G.C.9 (Volume of cylinders, cones, spheres); 8.SP.A.4 (Two-way tables); 8.EE.C.8 (Word problems with systems) 1. Apply Kepler's Third Law (T² ∝ r³) to predict orbital periods; 2. Compute volume of cylinders, cones, spheres; 3. Construct and interpret two-way frequency tables; 4. Solve multi-step real-world problems integrating year content. Kepler's Laws of Planetary Motion, Orbital Mechanics, Volume Formulas (V_cyl = πr²h, V_cone = ⅓πr²h, V_sphere = ⁴⁄₃πr³), Two-Way Frequency Tables, Joint/Marginal/Conditional Frequencies, Capstone Multi-Step Problems. Final project: Mission to Mars planning. Quarter 4: 4 Weeks (5/3-6/3) 1. Kepler's Laws project; 2. Volume quiz; 3. Two-way tables quiz; 4. Year-end capstone test. PhET: Gravity and Orbits, My Solar System. NASA JPL educator resources. MSOS Math 8 site: Cosmos unit. Hands-on: ball & string orbit demo.
