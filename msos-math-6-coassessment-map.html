Joint capstone projects with STEM · Glencoe Math Course 2 (Volume 1 & 2) · SY 2026–2027

← Back to syllabus
Interdisciplinary Co-Assessment. Four times a year, one project is graded by both teachers using their own subject's rubric. Students submit one deliverable; it earns one grade in math and one in STEM. The math rubric measures mathematical correctness, problem-solving process, and quantitative reasoning. The STEM rubric measures scientific understanding, inquiry & design, processing, and reflection on impacts. One project, two grades, two perspectives.
📦 One shared artifact 🧮 Math rubric (navy) 🔬 STEM rubric (gold) 🤝 Teacher coordination
Q1

Lab Math & Density

Mid-September submission
JOINT CAPSTONE — ONE PROJECT, TWO GRADES
Hawaiian Rock Density Lab

📦 Shared Deliverable

A single field notebook documenting rock collection, mass & volume measurements, density calculations with KHDUdcm conversions and percent error, and rock-type identification.

🧮 Math Rubric

A — Knowing & UnderstandingCorrectly applies D = m/V; KHDUdcm unit conversion; significant-figure rules in computation.
B — Inquiring & DesigningSelects appropriate measurement method (displacement); plans for repeated measurement to reduce error.
C — Processing & EvaluatingArithmetic is correct; percent error |measured − accepted|/accepted × 100% computed accurately for all samples.
D — CommunicatingTables and calculations use consistent units; written explanation of each rock's identification follows logically from data.

🔬 STEM Rubric

A — Knowing & UnderstandingExplains density as an intensive material property; understands why density identifies a material.
B — Inquiring & DesigningLab procedure documented step-by-step; potential sources of error anticipated before measurement.
C — Processing & EvaluatingData collection rigor; observed error sources reflected on; identifies whether observed percent error is "good" (under 10%).
D — Reflecting on ImpactsConnects density measurement to real-world applications: mineral exploration, material quality testing.

🤝 Teacher Coordination

Math class introduces D = m/V, KHDUdcm, and percent error in early September. STEM class supervises the lab measurements in the second week of September. Both teachers grade the single deliverable.

Q2

Numbers at Universe Scale

Mid-November submission
JOINT CAPSTONE — ONE PROJECT, TWO GRADES
The Great Pluto Debate

📦 Shared Deliverable

A physical hallway installation showing scaled planet positions (the math contribution), a printed scale-map handout, and a 1-page Pluto-classification position paper using IAU criteria (the science contribution).

🧮 Math Rubric

A — Knowing & UnderstandingCorrectly uses scientific notation; computes scaled distances using ratios.
B — Inquiring & DesigningChooses appropriate scale factors; recognizes that distance and diameter cannot share a single scale.
C — Processing & EvaluatingScaled distances and diameters are computed correctly; coordinate-plane map is accurate.
D — CommunicatingScale handout is clear; mathematical work shown for at least three planets.

🔬 STEM Rubric

A — Knowing & UnderstandingUnderstands the IAU definition of a planet; explains why Pluto was reclassified.
B — Inquiring & DesigningEvidence-based argument structure; cites observational and orbital data.
C — Processing & EvaluatingCounter-arguments addressed; data accurately interpreted.
D — Reflecting on ImpactsConsiders how definitions in science evolve as knowledge grows.

🤝 Teacher Coordination

Math leads scale-factor calculations in October. STEM hosts the in-class debate the week before submission. Both teachers grade the physical installation and the position paper.

Q3

Reading the Earth

Mid-February submission
JOINT CAPSTONE — ONE PROJECT, TWO GRADES
Ages of the Earth Lab & Presentation

📦 Shared Deliverable

Lab report with M&M decay simulation, 5 dated rock samples computed using half-life formulas, and a presentation placing those samples on the geologic timeline.

🧮 Math Rubric

A — Knowing & UnderstandingCorrectly applies N = N₀(1/2)^(t/T); fluent with powers of 10.
B — Inquiring & DesigningM&M simulation set up correctly; data graphed and fit to exponential decay.
C — Processing & EvaluatingHalf-life calculations yield correct ages in scientific notation; comparison to known Hawaiian rock ages is reasoned.
D — CommunicatingMath notation is consistent; calculations clearly shown.

🔬 STEM Rubric

A — Knowing & UnderstandingUnderstands the geologic time scale and radiometric dating principles.
B — Inquiring & DesigningDistinguishes between different radiometric methods and their appropriate sample types.
C — Processing & EvaluatingPlaces dated samples accurately on a geologic timeline; interprets implications.
D — Reflecting on ImpactsConnects deep-time understanding to evolution, climate history, or Hawaiian geology.

🤝 Teacher Coordination

Math teaches half-life math in January using the candy simulation. STEM provides the rock-sample data sheets and supervises the timeline presentation in February.

Q4

Climate Data & Geometry

Mid-May submission
JOINT CAPSTONE — ONE PROJECT, TWO GRADES
Hawai‘i Climate & Energy Project

📦 Shared Deliverable

Interactive spreadsheet dashboard analyzing 30 years of Honolulu climate data + an infographic/presentation linking observed climate change to Hawai‘i energy choices and a recommended path forward.

🧮 Math Rubric

A — Knowing & UnderstandingCorrectly computes mean, median, range, IQR; calculates percent change.
B — Inquiring & DesigningData sources documented; appropriate baseline year chosen.
C — Processing & EvaluatingInequality analysis identifies threshold-exceeding years correctly; trends interpreted from charts.
D — CommunicatingDashboard is readable; statistical claims are accurately stated.

🔬 STEM Rubric

A — Knowing & UnderstandingExplains greenhouse-gas effect and climate-energy linkage.
B — Inquiring & DesigningComparison of energy sources is balanced; criteria for "best mix" are clear.
C — Processing & EvaluatingEnergy and climate data are processed accurately.
D — Reflecting on ImpactsRecommendation considers Hawaiian context, equity, and feasibility.

🤝 Teacher Coordination

Math owns the statistical analysis through April. STEM facilitates the energy-mix research and the final presentation in early May.
