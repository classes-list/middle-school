Unit 1: 🔬 Why STEM MS-ETS + MYP Sci MS-ETS1-1 (define problems); MS-ETS1-2 (evaluate solutions); MS-ETS1-3 (analyze data); MYP Crit. A/D 1. Apply the Scientific Method and Engineering Design Process; 2. Practice safe lab work and hypothesis formulation; 3. Investigate extinction level events (ELEs). Lab safety, variables and controls, hypothesis writing, lab report format; scientific method vs. engineering design process; connection to extinction level events (ELEs). Q1: 4 weeks
(8/3 – 8/28)		 ELE Poster (Crit. A/D); lab safety quiz. MYP Sciences Guide; Innovations Lab tools; classroom lab equipment; Dr. Eachus's Lab Notebook Framework.
Unit 2: 🌌 Our Universe MS-ESS1 + MYP Sci MS-ESS1-1 (Earth-Sun-Moon models); MS-ESS1-2 (gravity in solar system); MS-ESS1-3 (scale of universe); MYP Crit. A/B/C/D 1. Model universe formation and expansion; 2. Investigate matter (states, mass, volume, density); 3. Debate object classifications (Great Pluto Debate). Big Bang and cosmic timeline; star formation and life cycles; solar system structure; gravity; Kepler's laws; measurement (mass, volume, density); models as a scientific tool. Q1-Q2: 14 weeks
(8/31 – 12/18)		 Density Lab (Crit. B/C); Great Pluto Debate (Crit. A/D); Alien in Me project. NASA Space Place; PhET Gravity & Orbits; digital balances; graduated cylinders; class density kits.
Unit 3: 🌍 How the Earth Was Made MS-ESS1 + MS-ESS2 + MYP Sci MS-ESS1-4 (Earth's 4.6B-year history); MS-ESS2-2 (Earth surface change); MS-ESS2-3 (plate motion evidence); MYP Crit. A/B/C/D 1. Walk 4.5 billion years of Earth's timeline; 2. Read rocks and fossils as evidence; 3. Investigate The Great Oxygenation Event. 25 key events in Earth's history; rock types and formation; sedimentary layers as timelines; fossils and radiometric dating; The Great Oxygenation Event; Hawaiian deep time walk. Q3: 10 weeks
(1/7 – 3/12)		 Ages of the Earth Presentation (Crit. A/D); The Great Oxygenation Lab (Crit. B/C); Earth Timeline Project (Crit. A/D). Hawai'i State Geological Survey materials; classroom rock and mineral kits; USGS educational resources; Bishop Museum resources.
Unit 4: 🌡️ Climate Change & the Low-Energy Future MS-ESS2 + MS-ESS3 + MYP Sci MS-ESS2-5 (weather patterns); MS-ESS2-6 (climate); MS-ESS3-4 (human impact); MS-ESS3-5 (climate change causes); MYP Crit. A/B/C/D 1. Distinguish weather from climate; 2. Identify factors driving climate change; 3. Investigate the energy-climate nexus; 4. Design climate-resilient solutions for Hawai'i. Weather vs. climate; greenhouse effect; CO&sub2; and temperature trends; renewable and non-renewable energy sources; Hawai'i-specific energy audits; climate resilience. Q4: 12 weeks
(3/29 – 6/3)		 Critical Lab Skills Test (Crit. B/C); Sources of Energy Presentation (Crit. A/D); Baseball Project (Crit. A/D). NOAA Climate.gov; NASA Climate Kids; Hawaii State Energy Office; Innovations Lab tools; classroom energy audit kits.
