Beginning of School - Lab Math Foundations CCSS 6.NS.B.2 (Fluently divide multi-digit numbers); 6.NS.B.3 (Add/subtract/multiply/divide decimals); 6.NS.A.1 (Divide fractions by fractions); 6.NS.B.4 (Find GCF and LCM); 6.RP.A.3d (Convert measurement units); 6.EE.A.1 (Whole-number exponents) 1. Fluently perform decimal operations; 2. Divide fractions by fractions and solve word problems; 3. Find GCF and LCM using prime factorization; 4. Convert between metric units using the KHDUdcm staircase; 5. Express very large numbers in scientific notation. Place Value, Decimal Operations, Fraction Division, Factors & Multiples (GCF/LCM), Metric Unit Conversions (KHDUdcm), Scientific Notation. Activity: Lab Math Bootcamp — measure mass and volume of small objects, convert units, record in decimal & scientific notation. Quarter 1: 5 Weeks (8/3-9/4) 1. Decimal operations quiz; 2. Fraction division quiz; 3. Unit conversion worksheet; 4. Scientific notation quiz; 5. Lab Math Bootcamp report. PhET: Arithmetic, Fraction Matcher, Number Line. Khan Academy: 6th grade arithmetic. MSOS Math 6 site: Lab Math units. Hands-on: triple-beam balance, graduated cylinders.
Density & Measurement CCSS 6.RP.A.1 (Concept of ratio); 6.RP.A.2 (Unit rate); 6.RP.A.3 (Use ratio/rate reasoning); 6.RP.A.3c (Percent of a quantity); 6.RP.A.3d (Convert measurement units) 1. Compute density using D = m/V; 2. Calculate percent error in lab measurements; 3. Predict whether objects sink or float; 4. Identify minerals using density; 5. Convert between mass and volume units. Density formula (D = m/V), Percent Error = |measured − actual| / actual × 100%, Mineral Identification, Sink/Float prediction, Earth's layers (crust/mantle/core density gradient). Lab: Penny Density Experiment; Mineral Density Lab. Quarter 1: 4 Weeks (9/8-10/2) 1. Density formula quiz; 2. Penny experiment lab report (with percent error); 3. Mineral ID lab; 4. Quarter 1 test. PhET: Density, Buoyancy. MSOS Math 6: Density unit, Percent Error Lab. Hands-on: pennies (pre/post-1982), mineral specimens, graduated cylinders.
Ratios, Percents & Universe Scale CCSS 6.RP.A.1-3 (Ratios, rates, percents); 6.RP.A.3c (Percent of a quantity); 6.EE.A.1 (Whole-number exponents); 6.NS.C.5-6 (Positive/negative integers); 6.NS.C.6c (Plot integers); 6.NS.C.7 (Order rational numbers); 6.NS.C.8 (Coordinate plane problems) 1. Use equivalent ratios and unit rates; 2. Find percent of a number; 3. Use powers of 10 to describe astronomical distances; 4. Understand and operate with positive and negative integers; 5. Plot ordered pairs in all four quadrants. Equivalent Ratios, Unit Rates, Percent of a Quantity, Powers of 10, Orders of Magnitude, Solar System Scale, Integers, Absolute Value, Coordinate Plane (4 quadrants). Project: Scale Model of the Solar System using powers of 10. Quarter 2: 9 Weeks (10/19-12/18) 1. Ratios & rates quiz; 2. Percents quiz; 3. Solar System scale project; 4. Integers & absolute value quiz; 5. Coordinate plane quiz; 6. Quarter 2 test. PhET: Ratio & Proportion, Build a Fraction. NASA Solar System Exploration. MSOS Math 6: Ratios & Universe Scale unit. Hands-on: number-line ribbon, coordinate plane mats.
Algebra & Earth Science Mathematics CCSS 6.RP.A.3 (Rate reasoning); 6.RP.A.3b (Unit rate problems); 6.EE.A.1 (Whole-number exponents); 6.EE.A.2 (Write/read/evaluate expressions); 6.EE.B.5 (Solve equations as finding true values); 6.EE.B.6 (Variables in real-world problems); 6.EE.B.7 (Solve one-step equations) 1. Read topographic maps and compute slope as rise/run; 2. Compute plate-tectonic rates in cm/year; 3. Use powers of 10 to interpret the Richter scale; 4. Model radioactive half-life decay; 5. Write and evaluate algebraic expressions; 6. Solve one-step equations. Topographic Maps & Slope, Plate Tectonic Rates, Richter Scale (powers of 10), Radioactive Half-Life, Algebraic Expressions (variables, evaluation, properties), One-Step Equations (+, −, ×, ÷). Activity: Half-Life Penny Toss; Map Slope Field Lab. Quarter 3: 9 Weeks (1/7-3/12) 1. Topographic map quiz with slope calculations; 2. Plate tectonics rate problems; 3. Richter scale quiz; 4. Half-life lab report; 5. Algebraic expressions quiz; 6. One-step equations quiz; 7. Quarter 3 test. PhET: Equation Grapher, Expression Exchange, Plate Tectonics. USGS Topographic Maps. MSOS Math 6: Reading the Earth unit. Hands-on: pennies (half-life), topographic puzzles.
Statistics, Inequalities & Relationships CCSS 6.SP.A.1 (Recognize statistical questions); 6.SP.A.2 (Distributions); 6.SP.B.4 (Display data); 6.SP.B.5 (Summarize data); 6.EE.B.8 (One-step inequalities); 6.EE.C.9 (Two-variable relationships) 1. Recognize statistical questions and describe distributions; 2. Compute mean, median, mode, and range; 3. Display data using dot plots, histograms, and box plots; 4. Solve and graph one-step inequalities; 5. Represent two-variable relationships using tables and graphs. Statistical Questions, Measures of Center (mean, median, mode), Range, Dot Plots, Histograms, Box Plots, Inequalities (graph, solve), Independent/Dependent Variables, Climate Data Analysis (CO₂, temperature trends). Quarter 4: 4 Weeks (3/29-4/23) 1. Statistics displays quiz; 2. Climate Data Report project; 3. Inequalities quiz. PhET: Plinko Probability, Curve Fitting. NOAA Climate.gov data. MSOS Math 6: Statistics & Data unit. Hands-on: climate dataset, calculators.
Geometry: Area, Surface Area, Volume CCSS 6.G.A.1 (Area of polygons); 6.G.A.2 (Volume of right prisms with fractional sides); 6.G.A.4 (Surface area using nets) 1. Find area of triangles, special quadrilaterals, and composite polygons by composing/decomposing; 2. Compute volume of right rectangular prisms with fractional edge lengths; 3. Use nets to find surface area of three-dimensional figures; 4. Solve real-world geometric problems. Area of Polygons (decomposition method), Volume of Prisms (V = lwh; fractional sides), Surface Area using Nets, Composite Solids, Container Design Project. Final capstone: Multi-step word problems integrating all year's content. Quarter 4: 5 Weeks (4/26-6/3) 1. Area quiz; 2. Volume quiz; 3. Surface area quiz; 4. Container Design final project; 5. Year-end capstone test. PhET: Area Builder, Area Model. MSOS Math 6: Geometry unit. Hands-on: net templates, isometric paper, building blocks.
