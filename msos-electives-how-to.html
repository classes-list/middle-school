Course Overview

How To is a hands-on elective built around the practical skills middle schoolers will use every week — at home, on the go, and into adulthood. Across 10 sessions, students learn to do laundry the right way, fix small things around the house, sew on a button, manage their money, cook a simple meal, plan their time, and communicate with adults. Each session focuses on one skill, with hands-on practice, so students walk away ready to do it again on their own.

The course is offered twice a year — once in Quarter 2 and again in Quarter 4 — so students can elect it in whichever window fits their schedule.

Core Themes Woven Throughout

  • Safety first — using tools and materials without injuring yourself or others
  • Confidence through repetition — doing it once with help, then doing it alone
  • Reading instructions — care labels, recipes, manuals, package directions
  • Problem-solving — what to try when the first attempt doesn’t work
  • Money and resource awareness — using what you have, choosing what you buy
  • Asking for help — recognizing when a job is beyond DIY
  • Independence — doing for yourself what others used to do for you
  • Pride in workmanship — finishing what you start, and finishing it cleanly

Course Structure

Each session runs about 45–55 minutes and follows the same flow: short introduction, instructor demonstration, hands-on practice, and a brief reflection. Students take home a one-page “skills card” after each session — a quick reference they can pin on the fridge or tuck into a backpack for later use.

SYLLABUS 10 Sessions · 10 Practical Skills Offered in Quarter 2 and Quarter 4
1🧺 Laundry Done Right
DomainHousehold Life SkillSort fabrics, decode care labels, run a wash, treat stains Hands-onWash, dry, and fold a load of mixed clothes; treat 4 common stain samples ConceptsCare label symbols, water temperatures, detergent dosing, stain chemistry
Learning Objectives
  • Read and interpret care labels on different fabrics
  • Sort laundry by color, weight, and care requirements
  • Operate a washer and dryer with the correct cycle and temperature
  • Treat grease, blood, grass, and ink stains using household products

Instructor note: Pre-stage stained fabric samples in zip bags. Bring a real care-label cheat sheet.

2🧹 Clean Like a Pro
DomainHousehold Life SkillSystematic cleaning of kitchen and bathroom; choosing the right product Hands-onDeep-clean a mock kitchen station and bathroom station ConceptsAll-purpose vs. specialized cleaners, top-down order, microfiber, “clean as you go”
Learning Objectives
  • Identify which cleaning products belong in which rooms
  • Apply the “top-down” cleaning order to a room
  • Clean a kitchen station (counter, sink, appliance) without streaks
  • Adopt “clean as you go” as a daily habit

Instructor note: Pre-stage stations with intentional mess (food smudges, water spots, soap scum stand-ins).

3🧵 Sewing & Mending
DomainHousehold Life SkillHand-sew basic repairs that save clothes Hands-onThread a needle, sew on a button, hem a cuff, patch a small hole ConceptsRunning stitch, whip stitch, secure knots, fabric types
Learning Objectives
  • Thread a needle and tie a secure starting knot
  • Sew a 4-hole button using the cross-stitch method
  • Hem a cuff using a basic running stitch
  • Assemble a starter sewing kit to take home

Instructor note: Provide pre-cut practice squares with deliberate tears and missing buttons.

4🔧 Tools 101 & Small Fixes
DomainHome Maintenance Life SkillUse basic hand tools safely and confidently Hands-onHang a picture or small shelf on a practice board; tighten loose furniture ConceptsPhillips vs. flat-head, drywall anchors, stud finder, “righty tighty, lefty loosey”
Learning Objectives
  • Identify and select the right tool for a given job
  • Measure and mark accurately with a tape measure
  • Use a level to ensure straight installation
  • Hang a picture or small shelf securely, using anchors when needed

Instructor note: Use foam board or scrap drywall as a practice wall. Stock multiple anchor types.

5🛠️ Patch Drywall & Touch Up Paint
DomainHome Maintenance Life SkillRepair small wall damage and refresh paint Hands-onFill a nail hole; patch a fist-sized hole with a kit; sand smooth; touch up paint ConceptsSpackle vs. drywall mud, mesh tape, primer, color matching
Learning Objectives
  • Patch a nail hole using spackle and a putty knife
  • Repair a fist-sized hole with a drywall patch kit
  • Sand a patched area smooth before painting
  • Color-match and touch up paint without visible blends

Instructor note: Pre-cut holes of varying sizes in scrap drywall. Use a non-toxic, low-odor primer for indoor practice.

6🚿 Plumbing Basics — Unclog a Drain
DomainHome Maintenance Life SkillClear common clogs and prevent future ones Hands-onUse a plunger and drain snake; install a hair trap ConceptsP-trap, drainpipe, what shouldn’t go down a drain, when to call a plumber
Learning Objectives
  • Understand how a sink drain works (P-trap and drainpipe)
  • Use a plunger correctly to clear a basic clog
  • Use a drain snake to remove hair and debris
  • Recognize when a clog is beyond DIY and a plumber should be called

Instructor note: A clear acrylic mock drain (DIY or purchased) makes the inner workings visible — a major aha moment.

7💰 Money 101
DomainFinancial Life SkillBudget, save, and make smart money decisions Hands-onBuild a one-week budget; compare two purchase decisions side-by-side ConceptsNeeds vs. wants, savings goals, compound interest, checking vs. savings accounts
Learning Objectives
  • Build a simple weekly budget that balances spending and saving
  • Differentiate between needs, wants, and savings goals
  • Explain how checking and savings accounts differ
  • Estimate how savings grow with compound interest over 1, 5, and 10 years

Instructor note: Use realistic middle-school examples: allowance, gifts, lunches, snacks, savings goals like a phone or a trip.

8🍳 Cooking Essentials
DomainHousehold / Self-Care Life SkillCook simple, safe meals from a recipe Hands-onPrepare 2 simple meals (scrambled eggs, grilled cheese, or pasta with sauce) ConceptsKitchen safety, “claw grip” knife technique, heat control, reading a recipe
Learning Objectives
  • Wash hands, set up a clean prep area, and identify kitchen tools safely
  • Use the “claw grip” knife technique for safe cutting
  • Cook one stovetop meal start to finish
  • Read a recipe top to bottom before starting; clean as you go

Instructor note: Choose recipes that finish in 15–20 minutes. Reuse cooking-class equipment where available.

9🗓️ Time Management & Personal Organization
DomainSelf-Management Life SkillPlan, prioritize, and organize daily life Hands-onSet up a weekly calendar; build a study plan; organize a real backpack ConceptsCalendars, to-do lists, urgent vs. important, focus blocks (Pomodoro)
Learning Objectives
  • Set up a weekly calendar combining school, activities, and chores
  • Build a daily to-do list prioritized by urgent and important
  • Organize a backpack, locker, or desk so essentials are findable in 5 seconds
  • Use a focus technique (Pomodoro) to start a hard task

Instructor note: Ask students to bring their actual backpacks and a planner. Provide printable calendar templates.

10🤝 Communication & Etiquette
DomainSocial Life SkillCommunicate effectively in person, by phone, and by email Hands-onMake a real phone call to an adult; write an email and a thank-you note ConceptsTone, formality levels, professional vs. casual, body language and eye contact
Learning Objectives
  • Make a phone call to an adult, introducing yourself and stating your purpose
  • Write a professional email with proper subject, greeting, body, and sign-off
  • Write a handwritten thank-you note for a gift or a favor
  • Introduce yourself in person with eye contact and a confident handshake

Instructor note: Set up stations — phone (real or simulator), email (laptops), thank-you notes (stationery and pens), in-person intros (mirror or partner).

Assessment & Grading

Because this is a life-skills elective, assessment focuses on growth, participation, and real-world skill demonstration — not traditional tests.

CategoryWeightHow It’s Measured
Participation & Teamwork 30% Engages actively in each session; supports classmates; cleans up after themselves
Skill Demonstrations 40% Correctly applies each session’s target skill in hands-on practice
Session Reflections 20% Brief written or verbal reflection after each session — what worked, what would I try differently?
Real-World Application 10% Apply 3 skills at home over the quarter; document with a photo or short note from a family member

Classroom Rules & Safety

  • Listen to safety instructions before starting any hands-on task.
  • Use tools and materials only as demonstrated — no horseplay around sharp, heavy, or hot items.
  • Wear safe clothing — tied hair, no loose sleeves near tools or heat, closed-toe shoes.
  • Eye protection and gloves are provided as needed — use them when asked.
  • Respect tools and materials — return them clean and to their assigned place.
  • Ask before borrowing or using something that isn’t yours.
  • Help classmates without doing the work for them — teaching is part of learning.
  • Clean your station before the session ends. The next class deserves a clean kitchen, bench, or table.
  • Report any cuts, spills, or broken tools to the instructor immediately.
  • Phones away unless they’re being used for a specific session task.
  • Practice patience — most skills take more than one try. That’s the point.

Materials & Supplies

The course rotates across several skill stations. The school provides shared equipment; students bring a small notebook and (for Session 9) their actual backpack.

  • Hand tools: Phillips and flat-head screwdrivers, hammers, levels, tape measures, stud finder
  • Sewing kits: needles, threads, buttons, small scissors, practice fabric squares
  • Drywall repair: spackle, putty knives, drywall patch kits, sandpaper, primer, sample paint
  • Plumbing demo: plunger, drain snake, hair trap (optional clear acrylic drain demo)
  • Cleaning supplies: all-purpose cleaner, glass cleaner, disinfectant spray, microfiber cloths, sponges
  • Laundry samples: fabric squares with care labels; pre-prepared stain samples
  • Cooking essentials: eggs, bread, butter, cheese, pasta, sauce, salt, pepper, plus a hot plate and skillet per group
  • Money 101: printed budget templates and side-by-side comparison worksheets
  • Time management: planner pages, sticky notes, colored pens
  • Communication: a working phone (or simulator app), stationery, envelopes, stamps
  • Safety: safety glasses, work gloves, first-aid kit on hand at every session
