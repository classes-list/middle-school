🏛️ SOCIAL STUDIES

Navigating the Pacific — Magellan & Cook by the Numbers

Math 7 + Social Studies 7, Unit 2 (Age of Exploration)
⏱ 2 weeks

🧮 Math Content

  • Coordinate plane (latitude/longitude)
  • Rates (knots ↔ km/h ↔ mph)
  • Proportional reasoning
  • Average speed across long distances

🏛️ Social Studies Content

  • Age of Exploration history
  • Pacific Ocean geography
  • Comparing voyage routes & their consequences

📋 Materials

  • World map with lat/long grid
  • Ruler
  • Calculator
  • Reference: Magellan 1519–1522 route; Cook 1768–1771 route

👣 Step-by-Step

  1. Plot Magellan's voyage. Spain → South America → through the Strait → Philippines. Mark coordinates of 6 key waypoints.
  2. Compute total distance. Use the world map's scale or great-circle approximations. Express in km and in nautical miles (nm).
  3. Look up duration. Magellan's voyage took 1,082 days (most of it without him — he died in the Philippines).
  4. Compute average speed. Total nm ÷ total days = nm/day. Convert to knots (knot = nm/hour, assuming 24-hour sailing average).
  5. Convert units. Knots → km/h → mph using your unit-conversion skills.
  6. Repeat for Cook's first voyage. 1768–1771, ~3 years, around the world via Pacific.
  7. Compare. Which voyage was faster relative to its distance? Make a table.
  8. Hawai‘i-centered map. Plot both voyages on a coordinate plane with Honolulu as the origin. Compare distances from Hawai‘i.

📦 Deliverable

Annotated world map (with lat/long markers) + travel-speed comparison table + 2-page essay on what the math reveals about exploration risk.

📖 ENGLISH LANGUAGE ARTS

Underground Railroad — Speed & Distance of Freedom

Math 7 + ELA 7, Unit 2 (Stealing Freedom)
⏱ 1.5 weeks

🧮 Math Content

  • Rates (mph, miles per night)
  • Distance = speed × time
  • Coordinate plane plotting
  • Proportional reasoning

📖 English Language Arts Content

  • Stealing Freedom novel
  • Harriet Tubman & the historical Underground Railroad
  • Empathy through quantitative reflection

📋 Materials

  • U.S. map with state borders
  • Ruler
  • Calculator
  • Reference: Harriet Tubman led ~70 enslaved people to freedom in 13 rescue trips

👣 Step-by-Step

  1. Pull the historical facts. Harriet Tubman walked at night (4–8 hours per night), at roughly 3–7 mph, for distances of 80–100 miles per trip.
  2. Compute hours of walking per trip. If trip is 90 miles at 5 mph, time = distance ÷ speed = 18 hours of actual walking.
  3. Compute nights required. Assuming 6 hours of safe walking per night, 18 ÷ 6 = 3 nights per trip.
  4. Compute total distance traveled. 13 trips × ~90 miles = 1,170+ miles on foot at night.
  5. Plot on a coordinate plane. Use a map with origin at Maryland; mark each major Underground Railroad stop (Wilmington, Philadelphia, New York, St. Catharines).
  6. Compare routes. Coastal route vs. Quaker route. Which had fewer miles? Which was safer? Math vs. risk.
  7. Connect to the novel. Reference specific moments from Stealing Freedom where speed, distance, or hiding time were dramatic stakes.
  8. Write the reflection. 2-page essay: how does the math of distance and time deepen your understanding of the courage involved?

📦 Deliverable

Annotated U.S. map with Underground Railroad waypoints + travel-time calculation table + 2-page reflective essay.

✝️ RELIGION

The Math of the Talents

Math 7 + Religion 7, Unit 3 (Christian Life — Parables)
⏱ 1.5 weeks

🧮 Math Content

  • Exponential growth (doubling)
  • Percent return
  • Compound interest analogy
  • Scientific notation for large values

✝️ Religion Content

  • Matthew 25:14–30 Parable of the Talents
  • Stewardship theology
  • Distinguishing literal vs. spiritual meaning

📋 Materials

  • Bible (Matthew 25:14–30)
  • Calculator
  • Spreadsheet software

👣 Step-by-Step

  1. Read the parable. Matthew 25:14–30. The master gives 5, 2, and 1 talent to three servants.
  2. Identify the math. Servant 1 doubles 5 → 10 (100% return). Servant 2 doubles 2 → 4 (100% return). Servant 3 buries 1 → 1 (0% return).
  3. Project compounding. If Servant 1 continued to double every year for 10 years, what would 5 talents become? Use 5 × 2^10.
  4. Express in scientific notation. 5 × 2^10 = 5,120 talents ≈ 5.12 × 10³.
  5. Plot the growth curve in a spreadsheet for years 0–15. Compare to Servant 3's flat line.
  6. Real-world parallel. What's the equivalent rate of return in modern investing? (Hint: 100%/year is wildly aggressive; typical stock market is 7%.)
  7. Spiritual reflection. What did the parable really mean? Talents = gifts of skill, time, faith. Was Servant 3's failure mathematical or spiritual?
  8. Apply to your life. Identify 3 personal "talents" (skills, time, blessings). Write 1 page on how to invest them.

📦 Deliverable

Spreadsheet exponential-growth model + 1-page spiritual reflection.

🌏 FOREIGN LANGUAGES

ʻOhana Statistics — A Bilingual Class Study

Math 7 + Foreign Languages 7 (Hawaiian values)
⏱ 1.5 weeks

🧮 Math Content

  • Survey design
  • Sampling & bias
  • Mean, median, range, mode
  • Percent of a whole

🌏 Foreign Languages Content

  • Hawaiian family vocabulary (ʻohana, kupuna, makua, keiki, hanai)
  • Cultural concepts: kuleana, mālama, hanai
  • Bilingual presentation

📋 Materials

  • Survey form (Google Form or paper)
  • Spreadsheet
  • Calculator
  • Hawaiian vocabulary reference

👣 Step-by-Step

  1. Learn the ʻohana vocabulary. ʻohana (family), kupuna (elder), makua (parent), keiki (child), hānai (adopted/chosen family), kuleana (responsibility).
  2. Design a class survey. 6 questions about family structure: ʻohana size, generations living together, % born in Hawai‘i, hānai relationships, weekly family time. Keep responses anonymous.
  3. Translate questions bilingually. Each question gets a Hawaiian word and an English explanation.
  4. Administer to the class. Compute response rate.
  5. Compute statistics. Mean ʻohana size, median, range, mode of generations living together.
  6. Compare to mainland averages. US Census shows average household ≈ 2.5; how does Hawai‘i differ? Why?
  7. Identify cultural patterns. Hawai‘i has higher multi-generational households and more hānai. What does that reveal about Hawaiian values?
  8. Bilingual presentation. 5-minute class presentation using Hawaiian phrases interwoven with statistics.

📦 Deliverable

Bilingual survey form + statistical analysis spreadsheet + 5-minute bilingual presentation.
