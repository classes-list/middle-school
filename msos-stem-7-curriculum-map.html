Curriculum Map by Unit

Each unit lists its standards, learning objectives, essential content, pacing, assessments, and resources.

Unit Standards Standards Reference Learning Objectives Essential Content & Skills Sequencing & Pacing Assessments Resources Used
Unit 1: 🔬 Why STEM? MS-ETS + MS-LS + MYP Sci MS-ETS1-1 (define problems); MS-ETS1-2 (evaluate solutions); MS-LS1-3 (systems interact); MYP Crit. A/D 1. Apply the Scientific Method and Engineering Design Process to biological questions; 2. Practice lab safety and lab report format; 3. Connect the natural water cycle to the human water cycle. Lab safety; scientific method; engineering design process; variables and hypotheses; Water Quality Lab; Hawaiian water cycle system. Q1: 4 weeks
(8/3 – 8/28)		 Hawaiian Water Cycle Poster (Crit. A/D); lab safety quiz. MYP Sciences Guide; Innovations Lab tools; Hawaii Department of Health water resources; classroom water quality kits.
Unit 2: 🧠 By the Dawn's Early Light MS-LS1 + MYP Sci MS-LS1-3 (systems in the body); MS-LS1-8 (sensing, storing, and using information); MYP Crit. A/B/C/D 1. Understand brain and eye anatomy; 2. Investigate perception and learning; 3. Recognize that learning capacity is not fixed. Anatomy and physiology of the brain and eye; sheep brain dissection; how the brain processes and stores information; individual differences in perception; scientific investigation design; mass, volume, density review. Q1-Q2: 14 weeks
(8/31 – 12/18)		 Technology and Perception Essay (Crit. A/D); Brain Memory/Learning Inquiry (Crit. B/C). Sheep brain dissection kits; PhET Neuron simulation; Innovations Lab microscopes; classroom perception activities.
Unit 3: 🦠 An Invisible Army MS-LS1 + MYP Sci MS-LS1-1 (cells as building blocks); MS-LS1-2 (organelle function); MYP Crit. A/B/C/D 1. Distinguish plant, animal, and bacterial cells; 2. Investigate osmosis; 3. Compare beneficial and harmful bacteria; 4. Debate the human vs. bacteria survival question. Cell theory; organelle structure and function; plant, animal, and bacterial cell differences; osmosis and diffusion; respiration and combustion; bacteria and viruses. Q3: 10 weeks
(1/7 – 3/12)		 Cell Analogy Poster (Crit. A/D); Osmosis Lab (Crit. B/C); Virus Debate (Crit. A/D). Classroom microscopes; prepared cell slides; PhET Osmosis simulation; Bishop Museum microbe resources.
Unit 4: 🧬 Body Systems, Genetics & Reproduction MS-LS1 + MS-LS3 + MS-LS4 + MYP Sci MS-LS1-3 (body systems); MS-LS3-1 (inherited traits); MS-LS3-2 (sexual reproduction); MS-LS4-4 (natural selection); MYP Crit. A/B/C/D 1. Map the major human body systems; 2. Apply Mendel's laws of heredity; 3. Model DNA transcription and protein synthesis; 4. Debate evolution and faith. Human body systems (circulatory, respiratory, digestive, nervous); Mendelian genetics; Punnett squares; DNA/RNA and transcription; protein synthesis; evidence for evolution; Rice Babies genetics simulation. Q4: 12 weeks
(3/29 – 6/3)		 Rice Babies (Crit. A/D); Blood Lab (Crit. B/C); Sesame Street Genetics (Crit. A/D). Rice Babies genetics kits; Blood typing kits; PhET Natural Selection; Punnett square templates.
